Imagine a vibrant, interactive webpage for kids, built upon a captivating dark gradient background that subtly shifts between deep blues and purples, providing a sophisticated yet playful canvas. Dominating the center of the screen is a large, eye-catching display area, shaped like a softly rounded, glowing card. This central card uses a glassmorphic style, appearing as a semi-transparent pane of frosted glass with a gentle inner shadow, making the current letter stand out vividly in a bold, rounded, and highly readable child-friendly font. The letter itself is rendered in a bright, neon pastel color, like a sunny yellow or an electric pink, making it irresistible to the eye.

Below this central display, arranged neatly in a spacious grid, are exactly twenty-six individual buttons, each representing a letter from A to Z. These buttons also adopt the same rounded, glassmorphic card style, featuring subtle translucency and a soft shadow that lifts them slightly from the dark background. Each button is a different, cheerful neon pastel color – imagine a row of glowing aqua, lime green, fuchsia, and tangerine hues – making the entire grid a rainbow of interactive elements. The letters within these buttons use the same playful yet clear font as the central display, ensuring easy recognition for young learners.

In the bottom right corner, or perhaps clearly aligned below the letter grid, is a 'Next Letter' button. This too is designed as a rounded, glassmorphic card, perhaps slightly larger or with a more pronounced neon glow to draw attention, colored in an inviting bright orange or vibrant teal. Its position is intuitive, guiding the child to progress through the alphabet.

When a child clicks on any of the letter buttons in the grid, a quick and delightful animation occurs. The selected button doesn't just change; it might perform a gentle, bouncy pulse, as if inflating and then settling back into place, accompanied by a soft, cheerful glow that briefly intensifies before fading, confirming the interaction in a fun, tactile way. This visual feedback makes the learning process engaging and rewarding, tying together the ultra-modern aesthetics with a truly child-centric user experience.
